Yesterday, the presid;nt directed the Attorney General to take
actions to strengthen criminal law enforcement against child
support debtors.   First, the President instructed the Attorney
General to convene a task force of federal, state, and local
prosecutors to enhance criminal prosecutions of child support
debtors.  Second, the President directed the Attorney General to
issue guidance on sentencing to federal prosecutors, including
suggestions for using novel and effective sentencing options.
Third, the President instructed the Attorney General to draft
legislation establishing a felony offense for certain cases in
which a person willfully avoids support payments to a child in
another state.   Fourth, the President directed the Attorney
General to place on HHS' child support enforcement web page a
list of persons indicted under the federal child support law who
have fled from criminal prosecution.
