Administrator Sally Katzen testifies on Thursday, April 17, before two House

CONGRESS TODAY (4/15):

SENATE
The Senate passed (65-34) S. 104, the Nuclear Waste Policy Act of 1997, with
the text of the Murkowski substitute.   (2 votes short of a veto-proof margin)
  [SAP sent, 4/7: The President would veto the bill. A package of letters from
DOE,   EPA, and CEQ were sent to the Hill, 4/9; EPA letter reo standards sent,
4/151



Prior to final passage of S. 104, the Nuclear Waste Policy Act of 1997, the
Senate tabled (59-39) the Bingamen amendment to remove the O&default08 for
interim storage to be in Nevada if the Yucca Mountain site was disqualified as
a permanent disposal site. The Senate also adopted
(66-32) the Murkowski amendment to the Domenici amendment limiting nuclear
waste disposal fees.

The Senate passed (97-0) S. 522, to Amend the Internal Revenue Code, as
amended. The bill addresses unauthorized access to IRS files.

Also today, Senators and Daschle and Johnson introduced a changed to the
National Flood Insurance Act that would put new policies into effect in a
matter of 15 days rather than the current 30 days.  The Senators aim push the
bill quickly through both Houses.  We will give you an update tomorrow on their
efforts/progress.
