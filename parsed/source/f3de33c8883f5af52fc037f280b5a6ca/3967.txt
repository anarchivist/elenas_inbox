    Question:      How many people are now on the welfare rolls nationwide?

    Answer:        10.7 million people were on the rolls in May 1997, down from 14.1 million in
                   January 1993, a drop of3.4 million or 24 percent. This is the largest caseload
                   decline in history: in no other comparable time period have as many people come
                   off the rolls. The 10.7 million people on the rolls represent 4 percent of the
                   population -- the smallest percentage of welfare recipients in the population since
                   1970.
