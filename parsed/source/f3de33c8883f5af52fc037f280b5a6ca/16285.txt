Question:   You talk often about the success of welfare refonn, but what have you done to fix
            the faults of the welfare bill, as you promised to do when you signed it last
            August?

Answer:     I do believe welfare refonn is working -- the welfare rolls have declined by more
            than 3 million since I became President. The decline is in part due to the waivers
            I granted to 43 states so they can require welfare recipients to work and make sure
            work pays better than welfare, as well as the new efforts states have undertaken
            since August.

            The bipartisan budget agreement I fought so hard for restores most of the excess
            budget cuts which were attached to the welfare refonn bill last year by the
            Republican Congress. We've restored disability and health benefits for legal
            immigrants who are too disabled to work. We've created a $3 billion fund to
            create jobs for the hardest-to-employ welfare recipients, provided an enhanced tax
            credit for companies who hire long-tenn welfare recipients, and restored $1.5
            billion to the Food Stamp program. I hope and I plan to sign a bipartisan budget
            plan into law in the next couple of weeks.
