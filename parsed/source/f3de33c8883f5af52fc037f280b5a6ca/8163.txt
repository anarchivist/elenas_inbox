FROM:          JUDITH A. WINSTON

SUBJECT:       PRESIDENT'S INITIATIVE ON RACE WEEKLY REPORT
               JUNE 5 - JUNE 12



ADVISORY BOARD ACTIVITIES

        June Advisory Board Meeting. The next meeting of the Advisory Board is
        June 18, here in Washington, DC. In preparation for the Board meeting, the
        members will meet in subgroups on June 17. Each subgroup will be chaired by
        an Advisory Board Member:

               Dr. John Hope Franklin        Learning from and Addressing Different Voices
               Governor Winter               Leadership and Values
               Governor Kean                 Education
               Linda Chavez-Thompson         Economic Issues

        On June 18, the full Advisory Board will meet to discuss the issues raised in the
        subgroup meetings.

        La Canada High School. On June 2, Angela Oh spoke at La Canada High
        School in Los Angeles to 150 students about the Initiative.

        Offices of Administration and Management and Budget Diversity Panel.     On June 10,
        Angela Oh, Virginia Apuzzo, and I participated on a panel discussion about diversity in
        the workplace. We spoke about the Initiative and challenges related to race in the
        workplace. The audience consisted of approximately 150 employees from the Executive
        Office of the President. The panel is part of OA's and OMB's efforts to promote
        diversity within the EOP, and they will hold additional activities during this month.

     Congressional Luncheon. On June 11, you hosted approximately 20

DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE

Choctaw Nation. The United States Department of Agriculture's Office of
Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) and the Risk
Management Agency staff traveled to Hugo, Oklahoma for a working session
with representatives of the Choctaw Nation, local economic development
officials, and UNISYS, to finalize details for development of the model
demonstration site and to establish theoChoctaw 8(a) company. UNISYS will
serve as a mentor to this company for business establishment and for
in-depth technical support. RMA has been very active and supportive of the
project, bringing rural America venture opportunities, and has identified
contracts that will be directed toward this program. USDA's contract will be
awarded by July with work underway shortly after.

DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES


Initiative on Race and Health. On June 8, Surgeon General David Satcher
gave the commencement address at the UCLA School of Medicine.
Surgeon General Satcher discussed tobacco and HHS's Initiative on Race
and Health.

DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY


Dialogues on Race. On June 4 and June 8, the Department of Energy's
Oakland and Nevada Operations Offices held dialogues on race as part of
their efforts to support your Initiative. The round table discussions focused
on the vision of a just and unified America, educating the nation about the
facts of race in this country, and promoting constructive dialogue.

DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION


Bilingual Education. On June 4, Secretary Richard Riley of Education
wrote to Congress expressing strong opposition to the Riggs bilingual
education bill that would repeal the Emergency Immigrant Education program
and drastically revise the Bilingual Education Act. The letter discusses the
Secretary's objections to various civil rights and bilingual education provisions
in the bill and sets forth his principles for strengthening programs that help
students with limited English proficiency. The letter indicates that you will

SOCIAL SECURITY ADMINISTRATION


Federal Policy Roundtable Discussion on Minority Youth with
Disabilities. On June 17, the Social Security Administration (SSA) and the
Maternal and Child Health Bureau (MCHB) will cosponsor a federal policy
roundtable discussion to identify policies and programs that will improve the
employment outcome of minority youth with disabilities following graduation
from high school, college, and post-secondary employment-training
programs. The topic of the roundtable is the "Successful Transition to
Employment for Minority Youth with Disabilities." The roundtable will be held
in Washington, D.C., and will bring together more than 50 representatives
from SSA, MCHB, the Presidential Task Force on Employment of Adults with
Disabilities, the President's Committee 6n Employment of People with
Disabilities, the National Council on Disability, DOL, DOEd, HHS and
representatives of civil rights and disability advocacy organizations.
