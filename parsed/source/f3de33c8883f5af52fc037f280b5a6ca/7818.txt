MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

FROM:        JUDITH A. WINSTON

THROUGH: ERSKINE BOWLES
         SYLVIA MATHEWS

DATE:        MAY 22,1998

SUBJECT:     PRESIDENT'S INITIATIVE ON RACE WEEKLY REPORT
             MAY 16 - MAY 22



ADVISORY BOARD ACTIVITIES

      May Advisory Board Meeting on Administration of Justice. On May 19, the
      Advisory Board met at The George Washington University to discuss Race and the
      Administration of Justice. The meeting featured an address by U.S. Attorney General
      Janet Reno, who spoke about the importance of establishing trust in the criminal justice
      system, and included a factual overview of race and the justice system by Christopher
      Stone, Director ofthe Vera Institute of Justice. Professor Charles Ogletree of Harvard
      Law School served as the moderator, of the meeting, which included 10 panelists and
    . Advisory Board members Dr. John Hope Franklin, Reverend Dr. Suzan Johnson Cook,
      Governor Tom Kean, Angela Oh, Linda Chavez-Thompson and Bob Thomas. Among
      the topics discussed were racial profiling, jury composition, and disparities in sentencing
      between whites and minorities. Approximately 250 people attended the meeting.

     Religious Leaders Forum. The first Religious Leaders Forum was held on May 21,
     at Tulane University in New Orleans. It was hosted by Reverend Dr. Suzan Johnson
     Cook and was attended by more than 100 local and national religious leaders. Reverend
     James Forbes of the Riverside Church in New York City was the keynote speaker at the
     plenary session. The forum focused on the ways to strengthen the faith community's
     commitment to and involvement in, One America. The next Religious Leaders Forum is
     scheduled to occur in Louisville, Kentucky on June 1.

     Asian Pacific American California School Board Officials. On May 16, Angela Oh spoke
     to this organization in San Diego about the role of California public schools in
     promoting improved race relations.

     Ecumenical Group of Clergy. On May 18, Reverend Dr. Suzan Johnson Cook
           addressed the Ecumenical Group of Clergy for Metropolitan DC. She
     discussed her work as an Advisory Board member and her vision of One
     America.

    Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies. On May 20, Linda
    Chavez-Thompson was the keynote speaker at a forum entitled "The African American
    Military Officer in the New Millenium." The forum was hosted by the Joint Center for
    Political and Economic Studies. At the forum, the Joint Center released a nine-month
    study regarding the state of affairs of the African-American officer in the Army.

OFFICE OF THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

    Race Symposium. We participated in a symposium sponsored by the National
    Park Service and the Brown v. Board of Education Foundation. The symposium
    was part of a series of events that the Park Service has sponsored around the
    country on race. This three-day conference commemorated the 44th
    anniversary of the Brown decision. It brought together community leaders to
    address race issues that affect the quality of life in the community, schools and
    families of the people of Topeka.

    Dakota Territory Tribal Chairmen's Association Meeting. On May 20, we met with
    representatives from the Oglala, Rosebud and Yankton Sioux Tribes. The discussion
    focused on steps the Initiative could take to promote a more complete historical
    understanding of the Native American experience.

    Meeting with the Navajo Nation. On May 19, Advisory Board members Dr. John
    Hope Franklin and Bob Thomas and myself, met with Navajo Nation representatives
    Chief Justice Robert Yazzie and Sharon Clahchischilliage to discuss the May Advisory
    Board meeting and issues of concern to the Navajo Nation.

    The National Research Council Project. On May 19, we announced the National
    Research Council (NRC) project. The NRC will hold a conference October 15 and 16 in
    Washington, DC. The NRC has commissioned nationally recognized scholars to
    examine past and current trends for different racial and ethnic groups in key areas,
    including health, education, employment and the administration of justice and identify
    gaps in research and data that are needed to promote a clear understanding of race-related
    issues.
