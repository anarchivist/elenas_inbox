                                  THE WHITE HOUSE AT WORK
                                      Thursday, May 8, 1997

        PRESIDENT PUSHES FREE TRADE AND ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION WITH
                     LEADERS OF CENTRAL AMERICAN NATIONS

           Today, the President holds a summit meeting with Central American leaders to
    address the common issues of growing economies, good education, safe streets, clean air
    and clean water.

    ·       The President will sign an Open Skies Agreement that will increase the competitiveness
            and access of U.S. businesses in Central America.

          Earlier in the week, President Clinton visited Mexico, where he signed two historic
    agreements to battle drug trafficking and protect our common border:

    ·       "An Alliance Against Drugs," which commits both nations to cooperative efforts to crack
            down on corruption, money laundering, arms trafficking and distribution of illegal
            narcotics;

    ·       "A Vision of the Border 2010," which will help combat illegal alien trafficking and
            underscore the human rights of all citizens regardless of immigration status.

            The President's visit shows how far America's relationship with Central America
    has come. A decade ago, we focused on fighting insurgency. Today, we are cooperating
    to fight poverty, promote prosperity, strengthen democracy and protect the environment --
    making the Americas a foundation of U.S. security and prosperity in the 21st Century.


        YESTERDAY: FCC APPROVAL OF "E-RATE" A MAJOR VICTORY FOR EFFORT
              TO CONNECT AMERICA'S CLASSROOMS TO THE INTERNET

    ·       In a unanimous vote, the FCC approved the President and Vice President's proposal
            for discount education rates for schools and libraries that want to get connected to
            the Information Superhighway -- to help meet the goal of connecting every
            classroom and school library to the Internet by the year 2000.

    ·       This new "E-Rate" will make more than $2 billion in discounts available annually to
            schools and libraries. For most schools and libraries. the cost of both telephone
            and Internet access will be cut in half. For our poorest schools. access will be
            almost free.

    ·       Vice President Gore said: "This decision will help to ensure that all of our children
            -- whether rich or poor, from inner cities or isolated rural communities -- have the
            same access to the same vast world of knowledge."
