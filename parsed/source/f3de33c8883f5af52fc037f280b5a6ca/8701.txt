SUBJECT:    PRESIDENT'S INITIATIVE ON RACE WEEKLY REPORT
            JULY 11-17


ADVISORY BOARD ACTIVITIES

     Environmental Justice. On July 10- 11, Angela Oh and senior staff attended a
     conference on environmental justice in Los Angeles sponsored by the White House
     Council on Environmental Quality. The conference included a tour of toxic and
     industrial waste sites located near schools and communities of color, and meetings with
     industry executives, city and local leaders, and community groups to discuss options for
     resolving the environmental problems facing these communities.

     Race and Immigration Meeting. On July 13, Dr. Franklin, Ms. Oh and Governor
     Winter attended a conference on race and immigration in Washington D.C. This
     conference was jointly sponsored by the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace and
     the Georgetown University Law Center. Conference attendees discussed immigration
     and its impact on the nation's changing demographics, examined promising practices
     programs and participated in a roundtable discussion with journalists, community
     advocates and analysts. We expect the Advisory Board to follow-up with a letter to you
     in August.

     International Human Rights Meeting. On July 14, Governor Winter, Ms. Oh, Linda
     Chavez-Thompson, and I met with Gay McDougall ofthe International Human Rights
     Law Group (IHRLG) to learn about the United States obligations under the United
     Nations Convention on the Elimination of all forms of Racial Discrimination. Also in
     attendance at this meeting were members of the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights

    National Asian Pacific American Legal Consortium. On July 15, Ms. Oh spoke to
    summer interns from various government agencies during a luncheon at the National
    Asian Pacific American Legal Consortium in Washington, D.C. Ms. Oh spoke about the
    Initiative and the role of Asian Americans in the race dialogue.

    Kennedy Library Summer Institute. On July 17, Ms. Oh spoke about the Initiative to
    40 professors and graduate students at the Kennedy Library Summer Institute in Boston,
    Massachusetts.

    Corporate Forum. On July 23 in St. Louis, Missouri, Advisory Board member Bob
    Thomas and United States Department of Transportation Secretary Slater will participate
    in a corporate forum to discuss diversity in the workplace and linking majority and
    minority owned companies. St. Louis Mayor Clarence Harmon will deliver opening
    remarks.

OFFICE OF THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

    National Academy for Superintendents. On July 17, I was the keynote speaker at The
    Ohio State University's 1998 National Academy for Superintendents. Forty-five
    superintendents from 12 states participated in the Academy. I spoke at length about the
    Initiative and the administration's education policies aimed at reducing the disparities in
    the system that are attributed to race.

    National Association of Counties. On July 19, senior staff will be speaking at a
    breakout session during the annual meeting of the National Association of Counties in
    Portland, Oregon. We will provide an update on the progress ofthe Initiative and
    encourage county officials to be involved in dialogues on race.

    Race Discussion with Indian Youth. On July 13, senior staff moderated a discussion
    on race with approximately 70 Indian youth. The youth were from around the country
    and were participating in the United National Indian Tribal Youth annual conference,
    which was held in Crystal City, Virginia. Among the topics discussed by the youth were
    the lack of support for Indian programs in their schools and the failure of their schools to
    include Indian history in their curriculum.

    respectively, addressed the annual NAACP Convention in Atlanta, Georgia. Secretary

the plight of African American farmers, and on his civil rights initiative at the USDA.

Department of Interior

Central Arizona Project (CAP) Cultural Resources Repository. The Gila River
Indian Community (GRIC) and Bureau of Reclamation (BOR) reached an agreement on a
partnership arrangement for a cultural resources repository. BOR will contribute Central
Arizona Project (CAP) cultural resource funds to GRIC, to be used in concert with tribal
funds, for the design and construction of repository facilities on the GRIC reservation.
The facilities will be used to store and curate the archaeological artifacts collected by
BOR during construction of the CAP, as well as artifacts collected by GRIC from its
lands. A museum will also be constructed. At the end of 10 years, GRIC will assume
full responsibility for the long-term costs of the collection and its curatorship.

Department of Agriculture

Outreach Fairs. Beginning July 16, Department officials in Tennessee will host
Customer Service Fairs in Springfield, Brownsville, and Greeneville, each targeted to
reach small, limited resource, and minority farmers.

Catawba Indian Nation Partnership. The partnership between USDA in South
Carolina and the Catawba Indian Nation was officially sealed through a Cooperative
Working Agreement that will strengthen the Catawba Indian Nation's natural resource
and conservation initiatives.

Department of Health and Human Services

General of the United States was interviewed on Night Talk, an African-American public
affairs talk show broadcast on the American Urban Radio Network. Dr. Satcher talked
about the recent Boston race town hall meeting, the HHS racial and ethnic health
disparities initiative and the recent WorId AIDS conference in Geneva.

Minority Women's Health Panel. On July 23, Secretary Shalala will meet with the
Minority Women's Health Panel in Washington, D.C.

Department of Housing and Urban Development

Hispanic Serving Community Colleges. In a July 10 meeting with leaders of
national Hispanic organizations, Secretary Cuomo announced $3 million in HUD
grants to assist 114 students at 13 community colleges that serve significant numbers
of Hispanic students. The HUD program, called the Hispanic-Serving Institutions
Work Study Program, assists students hoping to establish careers in community
service. All students receiving grants under the program are from low-income
families and many are Hispanic.
Environmental Protection Agency

Title VI of the Civil Rights Act. On July 17, Administrator Carol Browner will
attend a mayors meeting in Detroit hosted by Detroit Mayor Archer to discuss
EPA's activities under Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Detroit Mayor
Archer is hosting this meeting to follow up the U.S. Conference of Mayors meeting
in Reno where the mayors approved a resolution calling on EPA to suspend its Title
VI interim guidance and to work with the Conference, and other stakeholders, to
address concerns regarding economic redevelopment in cities.

Office of National Drug Control Policy

National Organzation of Black Law Enforcement Executives. On July 22, Director
McCaffrey will travel to New Orleans. During this trip, he will address a special
session of the National Organization of Black Law Enforcement Executives
(NOBLE) Annual Conference. This special session will be attended by
representatives from four major groups: NOBLE, National Black Police
Association, the National Association of Black Narcotic Agent, and the National
Black Prosecutors Association. The expected attendance is 1,000 persons. The
Director will also attend a working luncheon/briefing with the gulf coast HIDT A
Executive Committee.

Social Security Administration

National Council of La Raza. On July 22, Commissioner Apfel will address the
National Council of La Raza in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The theme of this event will
