                                                  THE WEEK AHEAD
                                                    April 20 - April 26
                                            (All items are subject to change.)


             Monday (4/20): Possible statement TBD.

             Tuesday (4/21): President Clinton will meet with Democratic Congressional leaders to
             discuss legislative strategy. The President will make a brief statement following his
             meeting on the importance of the Senate vote concerning school modernization.

             Wednesday (4/22): President Clinton and Vice President Gore will travel to Harper's Ferry
             National Historic Park, West Virginia, to participate in an Earth Day service project on the
             Appalachian Trail. Following the President's service, he will make remarks emphasizing the
             themes of service, environment and the millennium.

             In the evening the President will reiterate some of these themes when he hosts the third
             Millennium Evening with the First Lady in the East Room. The subject of this Millennium
             Evening is "A celebration of American creativity through poetry featuring Poets Laureate Robert
             Pinsky, Robert Haas and Rita Dove."

             Thursday (4/23): President Clinton will hold a child care event in the Rose Garden, where he
             will receive a report on child care and its importance to the economy from Secretary Rubin and
             the working group of CEOs that Secretary Rubin has been working with. The President will
             also meet with President Niyazov of Turkmenistan.

             Friday (4/24): The President will hold a Cabinet Room meeting with school violence experts,
             academics, Attorney General Reno, Secretary Riley, the Vice President and First Lady on school
             safety. In the meeting the President will announce the Gun Free Schools report.

             President Clinton holds an event honoring the National Teacher of the Year in the Rose
             Garden to highlight his education agenda, specifically his master teachers proposal.

             Saturday (4/25): President Clinton will deliver a live radio address tentatively scheduled to
             focus on efforts to address fraud in a variety of areas including social security, Medicare
             and food stamps.

             Sunday (4/26): No message event scheduled.
