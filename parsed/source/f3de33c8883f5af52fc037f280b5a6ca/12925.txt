Constitution of and statutes of the United States, it is ordered as follows:

Section 1. It is the policy of the Government of the United States to treat all persons equally
under law in all phases of criminal enforcement, including questioning, detentions, arrests,
prosecutions, and sentencing. No person shall be subject to any criminal enforcement action
solely because of race or ethnicity. No persons shall be subject to any criminal enforcement
action motivated by bias or prejudice.

Section 2. It is the policy of the Government that the race of an individual shall be considered, if·
at all, only under circumstances in which it is essential for the effective administration and
enforcement of the criminal laws, such as the identification and apprehension of suspects whose
race or ethnicity is relevant in such law enforcement action based on reliable evidence.
Employees of the Executive Branch responsible for law enforcement shall engage in periodic
training regarding the circumstances under which race can be considered in particular law
enforcement actions.

Section 3. Evaluation and Data Collection

        In order to comprehensively assess the actions of all employees of the Executive Branch
and to develop specific guidance and training materials, it is essential to review the current
policy and procedures of all agencies with responsibilities for law enforcement and to collect
comprehensive data regarding law enforcement actions that may result in discrimination because
of race or ethnicity. Consequently, it is ordered that:

       a) All agencies with responsibilities for law enforcement shall, within 180 days, conduct
          an internal review of all policies, training materials, and other guidance relating to the
          use of race or ethnicity in law enforcement and report findings and recommendations
          to the Attorney General.
