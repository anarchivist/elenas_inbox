MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

FROM:       JUDITH A. WINSTON

THROUGH             ERSKINE BOWLES
                    SYLVIA MATHEWS

DATE:       APRIL 24,1998

SUBJECT             PRESIDENT'S INITIATIVE ON RACE WEEKLY REPORT
                    APRIL 18 - APRIL 24,1998



ADVISORY BOARD ACTIVITIES

     Fair Housing Meeting. On April 23, HUD Assistant Secretary for Fair Housing and
     Equal Opportunity, Ms. Eva Plaza, convened a meeting on race and housing issues at
     Rutgers Law School in Newark, NJ to commemorate the 30th anniversary of the Fair
     Housing Act. Dr. John Hope Franklin, Governor Thomas Kean and I participated in an
     interesting and substantive panel discussion on housing discrimination and integration·
     issues along with housing experts and practitioners from the New Jersey area.
     Approximately 400 people attended the meeting.

     Santa Ana Community College, California. On April 20, Angela Oh spoke
     about the Initiative to approximately 200 people, including the college president.

OFFICE OF THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

     Meeting with CEA. PIR staff members met with CEA staff this week to discuss the
     latest draft of the fact book on race. There are currently 51 graphs on a range of issues
     including education, employment, housing, health and crime issues. CEA has also done
     a draft of the narratives to explain further the tables and graphs. CEA plans to send a
     more complete draft to appropriate agencies and Initiative staff for review in June.

     Statewide Days of Dialogue. We continue to work with the White Office of

    was a forthright and constructive discussion of such issues as access to health
    care and economic opportunity, economic development and affirmative action.
    Panelists included local community leaders, as well as the Mayor and the County
    Executive. The keynote speaker was Arthur Fletcher, former Chairman of the
    U.S. Civil Rights Commission.

FEDERAL AGENCY ACTIVITIES

    Department of Treasury

    Partnership with Hispanic Federal Employees. On April 21, the Department of Treasury
    signed a partnership agreement with the National Association of Hispanic Federal
    Employees.

    Department of Health and Human Services

    Minority Health. On April 24, the Congressional Black Caucus held a brain-trust
    forum on minority health. The Administrator of Health Resources and Services
    Administration addressed the forum on health professions training programs.

    Department of Housing and Urban Development

    Fair Housing Act. On April 21, Secretary Cuomo moderated the fourth Community
    2020 seminar, "Thirty Years of Fair Housing: Many Neighborhoods, One America."
    Martin Luther King III, President of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference;
    Emanuel Cleaver II, Mayor of Kansas City, MO; and Nancy Denton, Professor of
    Sociology at the State University of NY in Albany examined the history and role of the

Fair Housing Act and the work that remains to be done to realize Reverend Dr. Martin
Luther King, Jr.'s vision of a society, and housing industry, free of prejudice and bigotry.

National Conference of Black Mayors (NCBM) Partnership.    On April 17, Secretary
Cuomo and the NCBM President Emanuel Cleaver signed a partnership agreement
pledging HUD and NCBM to work together on a broad range of economic development
and housing initiatives to benefit America's cities. The agreement includes provisions
requiring HUD to provide up to $200,000 in technical assistance services to help NCBM
and member mayors benefit from participation in the HUD's programs.

Mortgage Loans.      On April 21, Secretary Cuomo announced that SFM Mortgage
Corporation has agreed to target $42.5 million in home mortgage loans to minorities and
low- and moderate-income families in the Dallas-Fort Worth area of Texas over the next
three years to settle accusations of lending discrimination. HUD estimates that more
than 760 minority and low- and moderate-income families will receive mortgage loans
and become homeowners as a result of the agreement.

Department of Energy

Meeting with the Presidents of the Historically Black Colleges and Universities. On
April 25, Secretary Pefia will meet with five presidents of the HBCUlMinority
Institutions Environmental Technology Consortium. The meeting will reinforce DOE's
commitment to involving HBCUs and other minority educational institutions in its
mission-related programs. The HBCUIMI Consortium is one of the most significant
consortia funded by DOE. The 17-member consortia of institutions are well known for
their significant role in training and educating the nation's minority technical workforce.
Department of Veterans Affairs

One America Conversations. On April 15, VA's Federal Youth Task Force
representative visited Georgetown Preparatory School and met with five classes of
seniors to discuss race, ethnicity, and cultural diversity issues. The students have been
following the Initiative and were eager to take an active role in the One America youth
outreach effort. On April 30, a selected group of these students will represent
Georgetown Preparatory School at the Veteran Administration's One America Youth
Town Hall Meeting.
