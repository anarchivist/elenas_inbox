DRAFT STATEMENT BY THE PRESIDENT



"I am pleased that today's decision by the Supreme Court in Bragdon v. Abbott
reinforces the protections offered by the landmark Americans With Disabilities Act for
Americans living with HIV disease and AIDS. The ADA was enacted with strong
bi-partisan support to protect Americans with disabilities from discrimination. The
Administration filed a brief in this case arguing successfully that people with HIV are
disabled whether or not they are symptomatic, and that the protections offered by the
ADA therefore apply.


I want to assure all Americans with disabilities, including those living with HIV disease
and AIDS, that this Administration is firmly committed to protecting them from
discrimination. Today's decision will assist us in fulfilling that commitment."
