                        Legal Immigrants and the New Budget Agreement
                                            5/2/97
                                                                           Automated Records Management System
                                                                                   Hex-Dump Conversion

     Question:   Will you fulfill your promise to restore benefits to legal immigrants who were
                 treated so unfairly in last year's welfare law?

     Answer:     Yes, we will. In this budget agreement, we will restore disability and health
                 benefits to legal immigrants who become disabled after entering the United States,
                  provide Medicaid health coverage for poor immigrant children, and extend the
                 Medicaid and SSI eligibility period for refugees from five years to seven to give
                 them more time to naturalize.

     Question:   Why doesn't this agreement reinstate benefits to all elderly non-citizens?

     Answer:     In the context of a balanced budget, we targeted assistance to the most
                 vulnerable legal immigrants -- children and the disabled. Since most of the
                 elderly on SSI are disabled, most elderly non-citizens will be helped by this
                 agreement.

     Question:   Will future immigrants be treated differently from those already in the U.S.?

     Answer:     Those immigrants whose sponsors have signed legally enforceable affidavits of
                 support will have their sponsor's income "deemed" or counted during eligibility
                 determination. Since the first of such affidavits are expected to go into effect in
                 May, that means future immigrants will be subject to deeming while immigrants
                 who arrived earlier will not.
