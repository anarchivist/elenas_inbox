 Jon --

 We may make the announcement later in the week.    In the meantime, here is
 a description of the program:

 Police Corps is a recruitment program designed to encourage college
 students and graduate students to pursue a career in the law enforcement
 field.  Participating students can receive up to $7,500 per year to cover
 education expenses. Upon graduation, students serve in a law enforcement
 agency for four years.   States apply to the COPS Office to participate in
 the program.

 The states that have been selected for this year's funding, the number of
 students, and the dollars allocated are (THESE ARE PRELIMINARY):

 Arkansas (10 students; $361,000); Kentucky (15; $532,500); Maryland (20;
 $910,000); Mississippi (45; $2 million earmarked in FY '98 appropriation);
 Missouri (15; $572,500); North Carolina (15; $597,500); Oregon (20;
 $926,000); South Carolina (20; $947,500); Texas (20; $756,720)   Each of
 these states are currently participating in the program.

 The following states are   new participants in the program for this year:
 Colorado (20; $865,000);   Florida (30; $1,595,500); Illinois (20;
 $677,500); Massachusetts   (20; $730,000); Oklahoma (20; $850,500); Utah
 (30; $1,595,800); Virgin   Islands (10; $465,000).
