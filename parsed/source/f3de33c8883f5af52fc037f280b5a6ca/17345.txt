                            TALKING POINTS ON
               PENTAGON REQUEST FOR SHARE OF TOBACCO SETTLEMENT



        * The issue raised in the letter from the Pentagon needs to be resolved in the context of the
        comprehensive tobacco legislation which is just beginning to be considered in Congress. There
        are a variety of important and valid uses of resources that may become available as a result of the
        tobacco legislation, and the Pentagon's points deserve deliberation and weighing in the context
        of the entire legislation. The Administration looks forward to considering these issues in a
        bipartisan fashion with Congress.



        * Although there are many competing options for use of possible resources that could come
        from the legislation, the President has mad~ clear that the chief goal of tobacco legislation
        should be the reduction of youth smoking. On September 17th when he announced his plan for
        comprehensive tobacco legislation he challenged Congress to "build on this historic opportunity
        by passing sweeping tobacco legislation that has one goal in mind: the dramatic reduction of teen
        smoking."


        * When President Clinton announced his plan for tobacco legislation, he listed five key
        elements that must be at the heart of any tobacco legislation. The elements are: reductions in
        youth smoking, including tough penalties if targets are not met; full authority for FDA to regulate
        tobacco products; the tobacco industry must change the way it does business -- including its
        marketing to children; progress toward other public health goals -- including reducing
        second-hand smoke; and protection of tobacco farmers and their communities. This is the
        President's framework for tobacco legislation and issues like those raised by the Pentagon should
        be considered in this context.
