DEPARTMENT OF INTERIOR

Internet Access for Bureau of Indian Affairs Schools. Working in partnership
with the Bureau of Reclamation and the Bureau of Indian Affairs, USGS has
connected 42 BIA schools and six BIA colleges to the Internet.

DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE

Choctaw Nation. The United States Department of Agriculture'S Office of Small
and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) and the Risk Management
Agency staff traveled to Hugo, Oklahoma for a working session with
representatives of the Choctaw Nation, local economic development officials, and
UNISYS, to finalize details for development of the model demonstration site and to
establish the Choctaw 8(a) company. UNISYS will serve as a mentor to this
company for business establishment and for in-depth technical support. RMA has
been very active and supportive of the project, bringing rural America venture
opportunities, and has identified contracts that will be directed toward this program.
 USDA's contract will be awarded by July with work underway shortly after.

DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES

Initiative on Race and Health. On June 8, Surgeon General David Satcher gave
the commencement address at the UCLA School of Medicine. Surgeon General
Satcher discussed tobacco and HHS's Initiative on Race and Health.

DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY

Dialogues on Race. On June 4 and June 8, the Department of Energy's Oakland
and Nevada Operations Offices held dialogues on race as part of their efforts to
support your Initiative. The round table discussions focused on the vision of a just
and unified America, educating the nation about the facts of race in this country,
and promoting constructive dialogue.

DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION

Bilingual Education. On June 4, Secretary Richard Riley of Education wrote to
Congress expressing strong opposition to the Riggs bilingual education bill that
would repeal the Emergency Immigrant Education program and drastically revise
the Bilingual Education Act. The letter discusses the Secretary's objections to
various civil rights and bilingual education provisions in the bill and sets forth his
principles for strengthening programs that help students with limited English
proficiency. The letter indicates that you will send legislation to Congress
consistent with these principles at an appropriate time.

    SOCIAL SECURITY ADMINISTRATION

    Federal Policy Roundtable Discussion on Minority Youth with Disabilities.
    On June 17, the Social Security Administration (SSA) and the Maternal and Child
    Health Bureau (MCHB) will cosponsor a federal policy roundtable discussion to
    identify policies and programs that will improve the employment outcome of
    minority youth with disabilities following graduation from high school, college, and
    post-secondary employment-training programs. The topic of the roundtable is the
    "Successful Transition to Employment for Minority Youth with Disabilities." The
    roundtable will be held in Washington, D.C., and will bring together more than 50
    representatives from SSA, MCHB, the Presidential Task Force on Employment of
    Adults with Disabilities, the President's Committee on Employment of People with
    Disabilities, the National Council on Disability, DOL, DOEd, HHS and
    representatives of civil rights and disability advocacy organizations.
