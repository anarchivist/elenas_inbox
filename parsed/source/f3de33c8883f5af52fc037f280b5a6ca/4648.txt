                              STATEMENT OF THE PRESIDENT

       Today, the Federal Bureau of Investigation released its final crime statisticS
for 1996, and the results should be good news for all Americans. Overall crime
is down for the fifth year in a row, with the biggest reductions coming in violent
crime (6%) -- and especially murders (9%).

      But the best news is not in the numbers, but the fact that communities
across the country are making steady gains in the fight against crime. Both the
number and the rate of crimes are at their lowest levels in a decade. And
reported rapes, robberies, assaults, murders and all types of property crimes are
down from the previous year.      Even the arrest rate for juveniles, which has
skyrocketed for the last seven years, has now gone down for two years in a row.

       Since being elected President, my Administration has worked with police,
prosecutors, school principals and community leaders on a tough, smart crime
strategy to attack violent crime. Together, we have fought for more police in
our communities, fewer guns on our streets, tougher punishment for violent
offenders and better opportunities for our kids. Today's crime statistics show
that, armed with these tools, we are moving in the right direction. Law abiding
Americans are finally -- and decisively -- tipping the scales of justice in their
favor.
