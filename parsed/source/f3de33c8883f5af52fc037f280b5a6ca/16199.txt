CONGRESS TODAY (7/11)
SENATE
Passed (94-4) S. 936 - National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year
1998
        [SAP sent 6/19: Senior Advisors Recommend Veto)

While under consideration, the Senate took the following action on S. 936:
o       Rejected (19-79) Feingold amendment to require the Defense
Secretary to recommend canceling one of three new tactical fighter
aircraft programs.
o       Rejected (43-56) Bingaman amendment to reduce the total amount
available for the space-based laser program by $118 million.



The Senate also passed by voice vote the following (5) bills:
o       H.R. 2018 - A bill to waive temporarily the Medicaid enrollment
composition rule for the Better Health Plan of Amherst, New York.
o       S. 423 - A bill to extend the legislative authority for the Board
of Regents of Gunston Hall to establish a memorial to honor Geore Mason.
o       S. 669 - A bill to provide for the acquisition of the Plains
Railroad Depot at the Jimmy Carter National Historic Site.
o       S. 731 - A bill to extend the legislative authority for
construction of the National Peace Garden memorial, and for other purposes.
o       S. 231 - A bill to establish the National Cave and Karst Research
Institute in the State of New Mexico, and for other purposes.

HOUSE
Continued consideration of H.R. 2107 - Interior Appropriations Act for FY
1998
[Floor SAP sent 7/10: Senior advisers veto recommendation over lack of
funding for NEA and over Ehlers amendment]

While under consideration, the House took the following action on H.R.
2107:
o       Rejected (155-271) Ehlers amendment which would have terminated
the NEA and created Department of Education block grants for the arts ($80
million for State art commissions and local school boards) .
o       Rejected (173-243) Klug amendment that would have increased by
$292 million the billD,s rescission of $100 million from the Energy
DepartmentD,s Clean Coal technology program.
o       Rejected (175-246) Royce amendment that would have reduced the
Fossil Energy R&D account by $21 million.
