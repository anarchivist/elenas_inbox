While under consideration, the House took the following action on H.R.
2267:
o       Approved (voice) Burton amendment that expands LSC disclosure

court) and stipulates that this information be available from local
grantees (rather than through the office or court records).
o       Approved (voice) Mollohan amendment that prohibit the use of any
funds in the bill to promote the sale or export of tobacco products, or to
remove or reduce marketing restrictions on the export of U.S. tobacco
products to other countries, except for restrictions which are not applied
equally to all tobacco or tobacco products of the same type.
o       Approved (396-6) Gilman/Schumer amendment regarding designation
of terrorist groups
o       Rejected (165-242) Bartlett amendments that would have eliminated
UN and related arrears funding in the CIPA and CIO accounts.


o       Approved (213-189) Hoekstra amendment banning the use of funds
for the Teamsters
election.
o       Approved (voice) Smith amendment that bans the State Department
from operating a "1-900" number for passport services.
o       withdrew Klezcka amendment that would have mandated that the INS
return application fees to businesses that submitted applications to be
fingerprinting contractors.
o       Approved (voice) Barr amendment that bans the use of funds in the
bill for research on the legalization/medicinal use of drugs, including
marijuana.
