SUBJECT:   LEGISLATIVE REPORT


CONGRESS TODAY (5/15):


SENATE
The Senate failed to invoke cloture on the substitute to s. 4, the Family
Friendly Workplace Act, by a vote of 53 to 47. Senators DO,Amato and Spector
crossed party lines and voted against cloture on the bill.
  [SAP sent, 5/9: President will veto)

The Senate continued consideration of H.R. 1122, the Partial Birth Abortion.
While under consideration, the Senate defeated (28-72) the Feinstein amendment
and
defeated (36-64) the Daschle amendment.
 [Senate SAP sent, 5/14: President will veto)

HOUSE
Approved H.R. 1469, the FY 1997 Supplemental Appropriations Act of 1997 by a
vote of 244 to 178.
  [SAP sent, 5/15: President will veto if the automatic CR provision is
included)

While under consideration, the House took up the following amendments:
o Adopted (227-197) the Gekas (R-PA) amendment to add an automatic continuing
resolution provision to the bill.

o Adopted (338-89) the Kaptur/Riggs amendment to increase WIC funding from $38
million to $76 million, offset by a NASA windtunnels rescission.

o Adopted (voice vote) a McKeon (R-CA) amendment to provide $650,000 for a new
higher education commission.  For four months the commission will study the
increase in post-secondary education tuition and make recommendations before
the upcoming reauthorization of the Higher Education Act.  The makeup of the
commission will be addressed in conference.

o Adopted (voice vote) a Dingell (D-MI) amendment to provide $300,000 for
Monroe County, Michigan for costs incurred by the Comair crash.

o A Thune/Pomeroy (R-SD, D-ND) amendment to transfer $500 million from FEMA
disaster relief to the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program was
adopted by voice vote. This funding would affect all areas affected by
disasters, not just those in the Upper Midwest. Debate on this amendment
addressed concerns on providing grants to privately owned for profit utilities,
a waiver of public hearings, a waiver on providing funds to those other than
low income families, additional regulations related to this funding, and.
potential problems related to relocation and the short construction season of
the Upper Midwest.  These matters will be addressed in conference.

o A Sam Johnson (R-TX) amendment was withdrawn that would have directed
approval of the Texas public assistance contracting out program.   Rep. Johnson
said he was meeting with Erskine Bowles on this subject on Friday.

o A Maloney amendment to restore $1.7 million for the FEC was struck on a point
of order by Chairman Livingston.

o Adopted (voice vote) a Traficant (D-OH) amendment to add a "Buy American"
provision to the bill was adopted by voice vote.

o Defeated (100-134) a Neumann (R-WI) amendment, opposed by Chairman
Livingston, to strike $2.4 billion of FEMA funding, strike the $3.8 billion HUD
assisted housing rescission, and substitute a $3.6 billion unallocated
rescission of non-defense discretionary funding (requiring the OMB Director to
make such reductions) .


     o Approved (345-74) a Diaz-Balart/Meek (R-FL, D-FL) amendment to extend SSI and
     related Medicaid benefits to the end of FY97 for legalized aliens.

     o Sustained a Smith (R-OR) motion to strike on a point of order a provision
     limiting Conservation Reserve Program acreage enrollment.

     o Sustained a Young (R-AK) motion to strike on a point of order a provision
     concerning Endangered Species Act compromise language.

     o Sustained a Bachus (R-AL) motion to strike on a point of order $1.6 billion
     of transportation trust fund contract authority cuts.

     o Sustained a Kolbe (R-AZ) motion to strike on a point of order a provision
     concerning the earmark for VA garage in Cleveland.

     o Approved (voice vote) a Barr (R-GA) amendment, which provides $2 million for
     a Law Enforcement Commission.

     o Adopted (voice vote) a Barcia (D-MI) amendment related to funding for Bay
     City, Michigan.

     o Defeated (115-305) a Neumann (R-WI) amendment that would have reduced FEMA
     funding by $1.7 billion.
