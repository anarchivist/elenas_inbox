Women's Health Update: Staff from the Department of Health and Human Services will likely
make final recommendations for mammography screening for women age 40 to 50 at the end of
February. The Department will likely recommend that not all women age 40 to 50 receive
mammography screenings, but that those who have a medical basis for such be covered by
insurance. This recommendation will be viewed as a positive step by women's advocates.
DPe staff are working with Ann Lewis and others about outreach and education.

Update on Coverage of Mastectomy: The Department of Health and Human Services will
clarify that the Medicare program will cover mastectomies when medically necessary.
Therefore, if, following a mastectomy, a doctor and patient believe it is necessary to stay in the
hospital 48 hours or more, the stay will be paid for by Medicare. An announcement of this
policy and your support of similar legislation for private health plans may be made in the State of
the Union Address.

These initiatives coupled with provisions to improve health care quality and increase funding for
NIH will create a strong package of women's health initiatives.
