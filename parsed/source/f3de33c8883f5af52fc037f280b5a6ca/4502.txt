    Q:   WHAT IS THE PRESIDENT'S RESPONSE TO THE PRINCIPLES FOR
         CONSUMER PROTECTIONS RECOMMENDED YESTERDAY BY LEADING
         HEALTH PLANS AND CONSUMER GROUPS?

    A:   We welcome the recommendations on consumer protections offered by this broad-based
         group of health plans and consumer groups. The principles they outline make a
         constructive contribution to the important dialogue on the ongoing need for strong
         consumer protections in a rapidly changing health care delivery system. These
         recommendations are consistent with the President's commitment to develop strong and
         thoughtful national quality assurance provisions for the American public.

         We believe that these recommendations will be helpful to the work the President's
         Advisory Commission on Quality and Consumer Protections is doing to develop a
         consumer bill of rights. The Commission plans to release the bill of rights later this fall.
         We look forward to working with this coalition, the Quality Commission, as well as
         members of Congress on both sides of the aisle to find the best way to provide the public
         with greater confidence about quality in our health care system.


    Q:   DO CONSUMER PROTECTIONS SUCH AS THE ONES OUTLINED
         YESTERDAY REQUIRE FEDERAL LEGISLATION? AND HOW WILL THEY
         BE ENFORCED?

    A:   We believe that there should be national standards to ensure that all Americans have
         strong consumer protections. The issue of how best to enforce these protections is
         currently being reviewed by both the President's Quality Commission and through the
         Administration's regular health policy development process.
