              MEMORANDUM FOR LEANNE SHIMABUKURO

              FROM:         Diane Regas

              SUBJECT:      Weekly Report

              Children's Health Executive Order: White House staff (DPC, NEC, CEQ, and OSTP)
              are continuing work to refine the Children's Health Executive Order. The order is
              designed to ensure that children are considered when the federal government takes
              action. The executive order, as currently drafted, contains three key parts: 1) a
              requirement that every agency give priority to identifying risks that may
              diproportiaonately affect children; 2) a requirement that agencies specifically evaluate
              the effects of their regulations on children, if the regulation may disproportionately affect
              children; and, 3) an interagency working group to coordinate a federal research agenda
              for children. The executive order will help to fill an important gap in our current
              approach to regulations by emphasizing that children are not just small adults and have
              unique needs related to federal policy making. We have convened several discussions
              with individual agencies and plan to call together deputies or principals for any further
              discussion in the near future.

              ISTEA: White House staff have been hammering out final issues on the
              Administration's ISTEA bill. Specifically DPC, OSTP and CEQ are working to craft a
              joint position between EPA and DOT on environmental provisions in the bill. We have
              made excellent progress in resolving brownfields and clean C!ir issues; the remaining
              question is whether the bill contains enough provisions that will increase energy
              efficiency and reduce greenhouse gases. Our goal is to finalize a bill in time for
              Secretary Slater's testimony on February 26.
