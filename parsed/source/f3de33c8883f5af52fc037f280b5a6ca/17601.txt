MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

FROM:         Bruce Reed
              Elena Kagan

RE:           DPC Weekly Report




        Tobacco: Congressional Movement on Tobacco -- Senator Hatch announced
Wednesday that he wants to introduce a "discussion bill" enforcing a national tobacco settlement
before Congress adjourns for the year. Hatch said the bill would be modeled on -- but not
identical to -- the deal negotiated between the tobacco industry and the state attorneys general.
Hatch said his goal was to craft a bill that could have bipartisan support. Senator Kennedy may
also offer a bill before Congress adjourns next month. Senator Lautenberg and James Hansen
(R-UT) introduced a tobacco bill Wednesday that would cost $494 billion. The proposal gives
cigarette makers no relief from lawsuits, which was presumably their reason for entering into the
June 20th settlement. A bill introduced last week by House members Chris Cox (R-CA), Scott
McInnis (R-CO) and Paul McHale (D-P A) would limit fees paid to attorneys involved in the June
20th settlement to $150 per hour plus expenses.

       Education: National Tests -- DPC and legislative affairs staff are continuing to work
with appropriators and House and Senate leadership to find a suitable legislative compromise.

        Education: Charter Schools and Vouchers -- On Friday the House passed a special
rule linking the bipartisan Riggs-Roemer charter schools bill with the HELP voucher bill, also
sponsored by Rep. Riggs. The rule, which passed 214 to 198, provides for separate
consideration of the two bills, but would automatically add the voucher bill to the charter bill
once both have passed the House. Although Speaker Gingrich spoke in favor of the rule, it was
opposed by 8 - 10 Republicans, with 2 Democrats voting to approve the rule. Earlier this week
OMB issued a SAP waming that if the charter bill were amended to include the voucher
provisions, senior advisors would recommend a veto. Further House action is scheduled for
Tuesday.
