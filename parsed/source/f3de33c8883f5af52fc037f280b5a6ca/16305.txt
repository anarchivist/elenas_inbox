SENATE
Passed (88-12) Joel Klein to be Assitant Attorney General.

Passed (100-0) Eric Holder to be Deputy Attorney General.

Passed (91-8) S. 955 - Foreign Operations FY 1998 Appropriations bill
 [SAP sent 7/16: Admin. Supports]

While under consideration, the Senate took the following action on S. 955:
o Rejected (31-61) Bingaman amendment that would have allowed U.S. citizens to
travel to Cuba for up to 30 days for family emergencies (sickness, death,
etc.).   The amendment would also have allowed U.S. citizens to send limited
funds ($200 per month maximum) to family members in Cuba for O&basic
necessities,OS and would have allowed the u.S. to join in a multilateral relief
force to aid Cuba in the occurrence of a natural disaster there.
o Rejected (22-77) Hutchinson O&Sense of the SenateOS resolution that stated
disapproval of the Most Favored Nation (MFN) status for China.



Began consideration of S. 1023 - Treasury/postal Service/General Government    FY
1998 Appropriations bill (stacked votes, including final vote, expected to
occur after 5:15pm on Monday, July 21st).

While under consideration, the Senate took the following action on S. 1023:
o Brownback amendment was approved (voice) that blocks a January 1998 pay raise
for Members of Congress.   The provision does not block the pay raise for
judges or the Executive Schedule. Later, the House adopted (voice vote) a
Hatch amendment to stipulate that judges are not linked to Members of Congress
on the cost of living increase.   0 Approved (83-17) Feinstein amendment (No.
927) to create a breast cancer stamp that would cost one cent more than the
standard stamp with the additional revenue going toward HHS breast cancer
research (estimated to be $60 million) .
o Adopted Thomas amendment (voice) regarding government agency provision of
commercial services for other agencies.
o Adopted (voice) a Lott and Daschle amendment (no. 931) regarding FEC.
