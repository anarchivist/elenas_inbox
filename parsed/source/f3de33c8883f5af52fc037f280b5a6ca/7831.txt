Today, H.R. 2400 - ISTEA Conference Report was adopted in the Senate
(88-5) and in the House (297-86), clearing the measure for the President.
An Obey motion to recommit with instruction to drop the VA tobacco
provision was defeated (190-195).

Today, Chairman Livingston distributed a draft 302(b) allocation forFY
1999.  In aggregate, it is a the same level of the Senate resolution and
the BBA for defense, non-defense and crime.

CONGRESS -- TODAY (5/22):
SENATE:
Adopted (88-5)H.R. 2709 - Iran Missile Proliferation Sanctions Act of 1997
        [SAP sent, 5/21: Senior advisers veto recommendation]

Continued consideration of S. 1415 - National Tobacco Policy and Youth
Smoking Reduction Act    [POTUS letter sent, 5/20: POTUS supports
passage as amended by the ManagerO,s amendment]

The Senate will be out of session for the Memorial Day recess from Monday,
May 25 - Friday, May 29. On Monday (6/1) the Senate will convene at noon
for morning business and at 2pm, will resume consideration of the tobacco
legislation, with votes postponed until Tuesday (6/2). Also, on Tuesday,
the Senate will also hold a cloture vote on nuclear waste legislation
after 6pm.

HOUSE:
Rejected (120-289) the rule to consider S. 1150 - Ag Research Conference
report. The rule would have stricken the food stamp benefit restoration
to legal immigrants.
 [Lew letters sent 5/22: Senior advisers would recommend veto if the rule
to consider S. 1150 allows the House to remove components of the restored
food stamp benefits and the savings provisions]

Continued consideration of H.R. 2183 - Bipartisan Campaign Integrity Act
of 1997
[POTUS letter sent, 5/20: POTUS supports Shays/Meehan substitute]

The House also appointed conferees to the IRS Reform conference report.

The House will be out of session for the Memorial Day recess from Monday,
May 25 - Wednesday, June 3.
